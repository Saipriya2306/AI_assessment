*   **Natural Language Processing (NLP):** This area of AI focuses on enabling computers to understand, interpret, and generate human language.

*   **Computer Vision:** This involves training computers to "see" and interpret visual information from images and videos, much like humans do.
    *   **Examples:** Self-driving cars that identify pedestrians and traffic signs, medical imaging analysis for detecting diseases, and quality control systems in manufacturing that spot defects.

*   **Robotics:** While AI is the "brain," robotics often provides the "body." AI is used to control robots, allowing them to perceive their environment, navigate, and perform tasks autonomously or semi-autonomously.

*   **Problem Solving and Decision Making:** AI systems can be designed to solve complex problems and make decisions based on data and learned patterns.
